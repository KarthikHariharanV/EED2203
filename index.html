What is a transducer ?
  1. Transducer is a device that converts one form of energy to another form of enery.
  2. A transducer provides an output quantity having a specified relation to an input quantity.
  3. In the field of instrumentation, the output quantity is an electrical or eletronic signal. So, we say that a transducer measures pressure, load, force etc

REAL TIME APPLICATION
In pulse oximetry, the device emits light, typically red and infrared wavelengths, into the skin. The emitted light passes through the tissue and is then detected by the photodetector on the opposite side. This process allows the pulse oximeter to measure the amount of light absorbed by the blood, enabling the calculation of oxygen saturation levels.
photoresistor or photo conductive cell:
Photoresistors are light-sensitive resistor that decrease resistance when exposed to light
Phototransistor
An optical transistor is a device that regulates the flow of light, similar to how an eletronic transisor controls electrical current.It's essential for optical computing enabling the manipulation of light signals

Optical transducers play a pivotal role across sectors:

      → Industrial Automation: They ensure precise measurement and control, enhancing manufacturing efficiency.
      → Modern Agriculture: These transducers monitor environmental conditions and crop growth, optimizing farming practices for higher yields.
      → Medical Applications: From diagnostic imaging to biosensing, they enable non-invasive procedures and accurate diagnostics, advancing healthcare.
      → Environmental Monitoring: Optical transducers aid in assessing air and water quality, contributing to environmental conservation efforts.
      → Telecommunications: They facilitate high-speed data transmission through fiber optic networks, supporting modern communication systems.
      → Consumer Electronics: Optical transducers are integral to devices like cameras and barcode scanners, enhancing user experiences in everyday technology.
